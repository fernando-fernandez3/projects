## Question Categories

### 1. Childhood & Early Life

- Where and when were you born?
- What's your earliest memory?
- Tell me about the house/place you grew up in
- What was your neighborhood/community like?
- Describe your parents - what were they like?
- Did you have siblings? What was that relationship like?
- What did you do for fun as a kid?
- What was school like for you?
- Did you have a favorite teacher? Why?
- What was your favorite childhood meal?
- What scared you as a child?
- What made you happy?

### 2. Teenage Years

- What were you like as a teenager?
- What music/culture was popular then?
- Did you have a best friend? Tell me about them
- First job? First car?
- What did you dream about becoming?
- Any teenage rebellion stories?
- First heartbreak?
- What was dating like back then?

### 3. Young Adulthood

- How did you meet [spouse/partner]?
- Tell me the story of your first date
- When did you know you wanted to marry them?
- Tell me about your wedding day
- How did you decide where to live?
- What was your first apartment/house like?
- How did you choose your career path?
- What were your hopes and dreams at this age?

### 4. Career & Work

- Tell me about your career journey
- What was your first real job?
- Best boss you ever had? Worst?
- Most proud professional moment?
- Biggest work challenge you overcame?
- If you could do it over, would you choose the same career?
- What advice about work would you give young people?

### 5. Family & Parenting

- Tell me about when you became a parent
- What was it like bringing your first child home?
- What surprised you about being a parent?
- What was your parenting philosophy?
- Tell me about each of your children - what makes them unique?
- Favorite family tradition?
- Hardest part of raising kids?
- What are you most proud of as a parent?

### 6. Life Challenges

- What was the hardest period of your life?
- How did you get through it?
- Did you ever feel like giving up?
- What kept you going?
- Looking back, what did that teach you?
- Is there anything you wish you'd done differently?
- What's your biggest regret?
- What would you tell yourself during that hard time?

### 7. Triumphs & Achievements

- What are you most proud of?
- Tell me about a moment when you surprised yourself
- What's something difficult you accomplished?
- When did you feel most alive?
- Best decision you ever made?
- Luckiest moment?

### 8. Values & Beliefs

- What matters most to you in life?
- How has your faith/spirituality shaped you? (if applicable)
- What do you think makes a good person?
- What makes a good life?
- What are you certain about?
- What do you still question?
- What do you think happens after we die?

### 9. Wisdom & Advice

- What do you wish you'd known at 20? At 30? At 40?
- What's the best advice you ever received?
- What advice would you give your grandchildren?
- What's worth fighting for?
- What's not worth fighting for?
- How do you define success?
- What does it mean to live a good life?

### 10. Looking Back

- When you think about your life, what stands out?
- What are you most grateful for?
- Who had the biggest influence on you?
- What would you do again exactly the same?
- What do you hope people remember about you?
- Is there anything you want to say to future generations of our family?
- Knowing what you know now, what would you tell your younger self?

### 11. The Light Questions (End on these)

- What still makes you laugh?
- What brings you joy these days?
- What are you looking forward to?
- Tell me about someone you loved
- What's your favorite memory?
- If you could relive one day, what would it be?

## Interview Tips

### Do:
- ✅ Start with easy, warm-up questions
- ✅ Follow up with "Tell me more about that"
- ✅ Allow silence - people often share the best stuff after a pause
- ✅ Show emotion - cry if you need to, laugh together
- ✅ Ask about specific memories, not generalizations
- ✅ Record multiple sessions if needed

### Don't:
- ❌ Rush through questions
- ❌ Interrupt their stories
- ❌ Make it feel like an interrogation
- ❌ Avoid difficult topics if they're willing to discuss
- ❌ Worry about perfect answers
- ❌ Edit out "mistakes" or "awkward moments" - those are often the most real

## After the Interview

1. **Backup immediately** - Copy files to multiple locations
2. **Add metadata** - Create the interview notes file (see template)
3. **Thank them** - Written note or call expressing gratitude
4. **Share preview** - Let them see a clip if they want
5. **Store properly** - Follow GitFam storage guidelines
